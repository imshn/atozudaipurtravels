Sukhadiya Circle

Sukhadiya Circle is a popular landmark located in the heart of Udaipur, Rajasthan, India. The circle is named after Maharana Pratap's loyal minister, Rana Sukhdev, and is a prominent tourist destination. It is a circular park with a large fountain in the centre, surrounded by a jogging track and a garden with lush greenery. The fountain is beautifully illuminated at night, making it a favourite spot for evening walks and a perfect place to enjoy the cool breeze. Sukhadiya Circle is also home to a statue of the great warrior king, Maharana Pratap, riding his horse, Chetak, which stands tall and majestic in the centre of the fountain. The circle is a bustling hub of activity, with vendors selling street food, souvenirs, and trinkets. It is a popular spot for locals and tourists alike, offering a lively atmosphere and a chance to experience the vibrant culture of Rajasthan. Visitors can sit by the fountain, enjoy the views of the surrounding Aravalli hills, and soak in the ambiance of this beautiful place.
