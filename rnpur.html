Ranakpur

Ranakpur is a small village located in the Pali district of Rajasthan, India. It is renowned for its impressive Jain temples, built in the 15th century, which attract visitors from all over the world. The main temple, the Chaumukha Temple, is dedicated to Adinath, the first Tirthankara of the Jain religion. It is famous for its intricate and exquisite carvings, which adorn the entire structure. The temple is constructed from white marble and is supported by 1444 intricately carved pillars, each one unique in its design. The village of Ranakpur is surrounded by lush green forests and hills, providing a serene and tranquil atmosphere to the visitors. It is situated about 95 km from Udaipur, another popular tourist destination in Rajasthan known for its majestic palaces and serene lakes. Together, Ranakpur and Udaipur offer a perfect blend of history, architecture, and natural beauty, making it a must-visit destination for travelers visiting Rajasthan.
