Featured

Fateh Sagar

Fateh Sagar is a large artificial lake located in Udaipur, Rajasthan, India. It is the second largest artificial lake in the state and was built in 1678 AD by Maharana Jai Singh. The lake was later rebuilt in the 18th century by Maharana Fateh Singh. It is known for its picturesque beauty and provides a stunning backdrop for the many palaces and monuments. It is also a popular tourist destination and provides a tranquil escape from the bustling city.

Sajjangarh

Sajjangarh, also known as 'Monsoon Palace', is a palace located atop a hill in Udaipur, Rajasthan. Built in 1884 by Maharana Sajjan Singh, it was meant to provide a stunning view of the artificial lake, Fateh Sagar. The palace, now a tourist attraction, is a testament to the grandeur of the royal era of Rajasthan.

Jagmandir

Jagmandir Temple, located in the city of Udaipur, India, is a stunning 17th-century palace-temple built on an island in the middle of Pichola Lake. Its intricate carvings, intricate paintings, and stunning architecture have made it a popular tourist destination. Visitors can reach the temple by crossing a bridge and can also enjoy boat rides for panoramic views of the temple and surrounding areas. Jagmandir Temple is an important cultural and historical landmark and is a must-visit for anyone traveling to Udaipur.

Saheliyo Ki Bari

Saheliyo Ki Bari is a beautiful garden located in Udaipur, Rajasthan, India. It was built in the 18th century for the royal ladies to relax and spend their leisure time. Pichola Lake

Pichola Lake is a scenic artificial lake located in Udaipur, Rajasthan, India. It was created in the 14th century and is surrounded by majestic palaces, temples, and hills. The lake is famous for its serene beauty and is a popular tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors every year. Boat rides and sunset views are some of the most sought-after activities around Pichola Lake.

Kumbalgarh

Kumbalgarh is a fort located in the Rajsamand district of Rajasthan, India. It was built in the 15th century and is known for its massive walls, which extend 36 km, making it the second-longest wall after the Great Wall of China. The fort is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular tourist destination..

Sukhadiya Circle is a roundabout located in Udaipur, Rajasthan, India. It is a popular hangout spot and tourist destination, known for its stunning fountains, gardens, and food stalls. Ranakpur

Ranakpur is a small village located in the Pali district of Rajasthan, India. It is renowned for its impressive Jain temples, built in the 15th century, which attract visitors from all over the world. The main temple, the Chaumukha Temple, is dedicated to Adinath, the first Tirthankara of the Jain religion. It is famous for its intricate and exquisite carvings, which adorn the entire structure.
