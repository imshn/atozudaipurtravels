Saheliyo ki bari

Saheliyo ki Bari is a beautiful garden located in the city of Udaipur, Rajasthan, India. The garden was built in the 18th century by Maharana Sangram Singh II for his queen and her friends, hence the name "Saheliyo ki Bari," which means "Garden of the Maidens." The garden is a stunning oasis with lush green lawns, beautiful fountains, and numerous pools. The garden is also known for its beautiful flowers, including roses, marigolds, and bougainvillea, which bloom all year round. The garden also has a beautiful lotus pool, a marble pavilion, and numerous shady alcoves, providing visitors with a peaceful and serene environment. The garden is a popular spot for picnics and family outings, and its beautiful scenery makes it a popular destination for photographers. Saheliyo ki Bari is a must-visit for anyone looking for a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of city life, offering a refreshing and peaceful respite amidst natural beauty.
